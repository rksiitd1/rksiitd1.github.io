Dear Dr. Tanusree Chakraborty,

As an alumnus of this prestigious institution, IIT Delhi, I feel compelled to address a matter of grave concern that not only questions the ethical fabric of our educational environment but also shakes the foundational intellectual rigor that our institution is celebrated for. This letter aims to unpack the unsettling experiences and the covert manipulations I and others have been subject to, courtesy of the "I Meditate" club and its unholy alliance with the Art of Living Foundation.

The Genesis: The Allure of the Happiness Program and Financial Commitment

I was introduced to the Happiness Program of the Art of Living Foundation in December 2021, a time when the world was grappling with existential questions owing to the COVID-19 pandemic. Your endorsement, Dr. Chakraborty, lent credibility to the program, encouraging me to invest emotionally and financially. I have spent approximately 30,000 to 40,000 INR in various courses and sessions, believing they would alleviate stress and usher in emotional well-being. In hindsight, I consider these investments a colossal mistake, one that exploited my intellectual and financial vulnerabilities.

The Structure of Emotional Exploitation: The Cult Mechanism

Upon closer observation, the Art of Living operates much like a cult, cleverly disguised in spiritual vocabulary and rituals. Members are emotionally manipulated into a belief system that's less spiritual and more indoctrinating. The common denominator here is emotional exploitation. They employ a variety of techniques to manipulate emotions, making one believe in the pursuit of unattainable spiritual goals, often referred to as 'Punya,' to keep members entrenched in a never-ending cycle of dependency and financial commitment.

Subversion of Intellectual Autonomy: The Dangers of Uncritical Acceptance

What is even more alarming is the aggressive promotion of religious rituals and dogmas that go against the very grain of critical thinking and intellectual autonomy. These rituals, framed as paths to ultimate happiness or enlightenment, encourage participants to cease questioning and surrender their intellectual autonomy. This is a grotesque violation of the essence of education, which is to foster critical thinking.

The Intellectual Fallacy: The Happiness Economy and the Mirage of Well-being

The underpinning belief that happiness is the ultimate goal in life is not just intellectually lazy, it's dangerous. This myopic view is amplified and sold as profound wisdom, and it caters primarily to an affluent class suffering from existential boredom. This happiness economy is nothing more than a marketplace for false promises.

Academic Accountability and Intellectual Crisis

It's disheartening to witness faculty members like you, Dr. Chakraborty, endorse such a program, thereby providing it a veneer of legitimacy. By doing so, you're contradicting the principles of intellectual rigor and ethical scrutiny that educational institutions like IIT Delhi are supposed to uphold.

A Deeper Look into the Academic Culture: The Irony of Intellectual Institutions

Even within the haloed corridors of IIT Delhi, not everyone is an epitome of intellectual curiosity. Many are focused solely on the instrumental benefits of education: getting a degree, landing a job, or amassing accolades without any genuine thirst for knowledge. This lack of critical thinking leaves them vulnerable to cult-like programs that prey on emotional and intellectual insecurities.

My own department: DBEB and the Institutional Loop

My own department, the Department of Biotechnology (DBEB), is no exception. An alarming culture of rule-bending prevails, further lowering my expectations of an institution that was once held in high regard.

Closing Remarks: A Call for Collective Action and Intellectual Integrity

This is not just a letter; it's a clarion call for intellectual and ethical reevaluation within our academic institution. While I may stand alone in this crusade, I bear the burden of representing a silenced majority that has been exploited or is on the precipice of exploitation. The time is ripe for initiating a rigorous intellectual debate and scrutinizing the ethical dimensions of such programs.

I earnestly await your response, hoping this will be the catalyst for sweeping changes in the intellectual and ethical climate at IIT Delhi.

Yours Sincerely,
Ratnesh Kumar Sharma
Founder, AIxB
Alumnus, IIT Delhi
Sarita Vihar, South Delhi
